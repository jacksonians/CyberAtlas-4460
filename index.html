    Understanding Global Cyber Preparedness

    What the data reveals

    The globe visualization displays cybersecurity readiness across nations using four key metrics:

    • CEI (Cybersecurity Exposure Index): Measures a country's vulnerability to cyber threats based on infrastructure and digital exposure.
    • GCI (Global Cybersecurity Index): ITU's assessment of commitment to cybersecurity across legal, technical, organizational, capacity building, and cooperation pillars.
    • NCSI (National Cyber Security Index): Evaluates a country's preparedness to prevent cyber threats and manage cyber incidents.
    • DDL (Data Defense Level): Measures the strength of data protection laws and enforcement mechanisms.

    Key Insights

    Countries with darker green shading demonstrate stronger cybersecurity infrastructure and preparedness. These nations typically invest heavily in:

    • Advanced threat detection systems
    • Comprehensive cybersecurity legislation
    • Public-private sector collaboration
    • Cybersecurity education and workforce development

    Why this matters: Nations with weaker cybersecurity (lighter colors) are more vulnerable to attacks, data breaches, and digital infrastructure disruptions. This creates global security risks as attackers often use less-protected countries as launching points for international cyber operations.

    The Speed and Lethality of Cyber Attacks

    How fast threats propagate through interconnected systems

    Modern cyber attacks spread at alarming speeds through interconnected digital infrastructure. What you witnessed in the network visualization represents real-world attack propagation patterns.

    WannaCry: A Case Study in Rapid Propagation

    4 hours to reach 150+ countries
    230,000+ computers infected
    $4 billion in damages

    In May 2017, the WannaCry ransomware attack demonstrated the devastating speed of modern cyber threats:

    • Initial Spread: Within the first 4 hours, WannaCry infected computers in over 150 countries
    • Attack Vector: Exploited a Windows vulnerability (EternalBlue) to spread automatically without user interaction
    • Critical Systems Hit: UK's National Health Service, FedEx, Renault factories, German railways, and thousands of businesses
    • Healthcare Impact: Hospitals cancelled surgeries, ambulances were diverted, patient records became inaccessible

    Why Attacks Spread So Fast

    • Interconnected Systems: Modern infrastructure relies on networks where one compromised system can infect hundreds within minutes
    • Automated Propagation: Advanced malware self-replicates without human intervention, spreading exponentially
    • Supply Chain Vulnerabilities: Attackers target weak links in the supply chain to gain access to multiple organizations simultaneously
    • Zero-Day Exploits: Attacks using unknown vulnerabilities spread rapidly before defenses can be deployed

    Most Targeted Industries

    Why hackers focus on specific sectors

    Cybercriminals strategically target industries based on potential financial gain, valuable data, and impact. Understanding these targets helps organizations prioritize their defenses.

    Top Targeted Industries and Why

    🏥 Healthcare

    Why targeted: Healthcare organizations store vast amounts of sensitive personal data, including social security numbers, medical histories, and payment information. Patient data sells for up to $1,000 per record on the dark web—50x more than credit card information.

    • Life-or-death situations force quick ransom payments
    • Legacy systems with outdated security
    • Medical devices vulnerable to exploitation
    • HIPAA violations carry massive penalties

    🏦 Financial Services

    Why targeted: Direct access to money and financial credentials. Banks, investment firms, and insurance companies manage trillions in assets and transactions.

    • Immediate financial gain through theft or fraud
    • High-value wire transfers and cryptocurrency exchanges
    • Customer financial data enables identity theft
    • Stock market manipulation opportunities

    🏛️ Government & Defense

    Why targeted: State-sponsored actors seek classified information, intellectual property, and strategic intelligence. Successful breaches can influence geopolitics and national security.

    • Military secrets and defense technology
    • Citizen databases for espionage
    • Election systems and democratic processes
    • Critical infrastructure control systems

    🛒 Retail & E-Commerce

    Why targeted: Massive volumes of customer payment data and personal information. A single breach can expose millions of credit cards.

    • Payment card information sold in bulk
    • Customer credentials for account takeovers
    • Supply chain access to multiple vendors
    • Point-of-sale system vulnerabilities

    ⚡ Energy & Utilities

    Why targeted: Control over critical infrastructure provides leverage for ransomware and potential for catastrophic disruption. The Colonial Pipeline attack demonstrated how a single breach can create nationwide fuel shortages.

    • Essential services must pay ransoms quickly
    • Industrial control system vulnerabilities
    • Geopolitical leverage and sabotage potential
    • Cascading failures affect millions

    🎓 Education

    Why targeted: Universities and schools have valuable research data, limited security budgets, and large attack surfaces with thousands of users.

    • Research intellectual property and patents
    • Student and staff personal information
    • Minimal cybersecurity funding and staffing
    • Open network policies for academic freedom

    The Value of Stolen Data

    Different data types command different prices on dark web marketplaces:

    • Medical Records: $250-$1,000 per record
    • Credit Card Information: $5-$110 depending on card type and data completeness
    • Online Banking Credentials: $40-$200 per account
    • Social Security Numbers: $1-$15 per number
    • Corporate Credentials: $500-$10,000+ for executive access
